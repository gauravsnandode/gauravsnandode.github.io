Case Studies

Three projects that show how I frame problems, choose methods, and ship results.

01 Healthcare · Logistics

Geospatial Demand Forecasting for Healthcare Fleet Optimization

Problem

A healthcare logistics client was over-deploying fleet resources in low-demand zones while underserving high-demand corridors. Static, intuition-based scheduling led to high idle time, missed SLAs, and mounting operational costs.

Approach

Built location-level demand forecasts using tree-based models with H3 hexagonal indexing to capture granular spatial demand patterns. Integrated these forecasts into a real-time fleet assignment engine with automated daily retraining pipelines to adapt to demand shifts continuously.

Business Impact

Measurably improved resource utilization and reduced idle fleet time across high-density corridors — enabling data-driven dispatch decisions for the first time. Retraining pipelines ensured sustained accuracy without manual intervention.

02 Operations · Optimization

15% Cost Reduction via Fuel Planning Optimization

Problem

A large logistics fleet was procuring fuel reactively — no forward planning, no route-consumption alignment. This led to cost overruns amplified by volatile fuel prices and driver-level behavioral variance.

Approach

Formulated the fuel procurement problem as a linear program (LP) with constraints on fleet capacity, route consumption, and depot availability. Layered econometric models to predict consumption patterns under different conditions, and behavioral models to capture driver-level variance.

Business Impact

Achieved 15% improvement in cost efficiency on fuel planning — one of the highest-ROI data science projects the client had seen. The LP model is now the standard planning tool embedded in their operations workflow.

Linear Programming Econometric Modeling PuLP / SciPy Python Behavioral Modeling
03 LLM · Evaluation · GenAI

Production-Grade LLM Evaluation Framework for RAG & Agent Systems

Problem

LLM applications were being shipped without rigorous evaluation. Teams had no way to detect quality regressions, measure retrieval accuracy, or identify hallucinations at scale — making production deployments a black box.

Approach

Designed a 6-phase evaluation pipeline: use-case definition → golden dataset creation → evaluator design (LLM-as-judge, NLI-based, automated metrics) → benchmarking → drift detection → real-time monitoring. Built retrieval metrics (MRR, Recall@K, Hit Rate) using Langfuse as the observability backbone.

Business Impact

Enabled systematic performance tracking across model versions. Detected retrieval drift before it reached production. Reduced hallucinations by enforcing retrieval-constrained response generation — giving teams confidence to ship LLM features faster.
